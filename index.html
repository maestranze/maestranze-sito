Spine Bianche, the city-laboratory.

In the 1950s, after the Sassi were cleared, Matera reinvented itself on the plain. Born of the study commission promoted by Adriano Olivetti and designed by Carlo Aymonino's group, Spine Bianche is one of the finest examples of Italian neorealist architecture — and today a calm, characterful place to stay, with free parking and everything a short walk away.
